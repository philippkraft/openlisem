Infiltration options

  • No infiltration = catchment is impermeable
  • SWATRE = 1D finite difference multilayer scheme for soil water balance based on the SWATRE model (Richards equations), needs complex database setup
  • Green and Ampt = 1 or 2 layer infiltration based on simplified Darcy equation (Kutilek and Nielsen solution, no iteration)
  • Smith and Parlange = 1 or 2 layer infiltration based on exponential solution of the Darcy equation.

Impermeable: lower soil boundary is impermeable and soil can fill up, or else there is drainage from the bottom of the soil profile and the soil is infinitely large. When SWATRE is used the drainage is the flux from the bottom node, when G&&A or S&&P are used, the driange is the estimated unsaturated conductivity.

2 layer (Green and Ampt or Smith and Parlange): use a two layer infiltration system, all infiltration maps needed for layer 1 and 2 (ksat1.map and ksat2.map etc)

Percolation (Green and Ampt or Smith and Parlange) soil water is removed from the lower soil layer by a flux based on ksat multiplied by an exponential decrease with soil moisture. When percolation is activated the moisture content changes.

Compacted areas
Cells which have a fraction of compacted area (compfrac.map) use the value in the maps ksatcomp.map (mm/h) and porosity porecomp.map (-) to affect infiltration in the top layer.

Crusts
Cells which have a fraction of crusted area (crustfrac.map) use the value in the map ksatcrust.map (mm/h) and crust porosity porecrust.map (-) to affect infiltration in the top layers.

Tile drains (Only with SWATRE):
Tile drains connect to the surface and transport water as subsurface flow. Uses kinematic wave.

Geometric average:
Calculate the average ksat between to layers as geometric sqrt(k1*k2), else calculate as plain average 0.5(k1+k2)

Fraction timestep:
Initial division of lisem timesteps to solve differential scheme

Profile table:
Text file with soil layer definitions, pointing to textfiles with soil physical properties (profile.inp)

Table dir:
Directory with tables with theta, h and k for different soil types.
