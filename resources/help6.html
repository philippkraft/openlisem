Advanced Sediment options

Two phase transport: sediment is simulated in two layers: suspended and bedload. Transport capacities can be different for each layer, see below. The water depth is divided into a bedload layer depth and a suspended layer depth. Suspended transport is based on the D50 (median) value of the grainsize distribution, bedload on the upper D90 (upper 90% grainsize). Detachment and deposition are reported as the sum of the two.

Multiple grainsize classes (not active for now): the sediment grainsize is divided into multiple classes, which are either estimated form the D50 and D90 maps, or used defined class boundaries are given. When this is selected, iption 3 for transport capacity will be chosen (see below)

Channel and surface flow

For the channel and the surface flow (overland flow and flood) the transport cvapacities and turbulence parameters can be selected separately.

Bed load transport capacity equation:
1-Van Rijn Simplified (2007)
2-Van Rijn Full (1984)
3-Wu, Wang & Jia (wil be used with multiple grain size distribution)

Suspended load transport capacity:
0-Govers
1-Van Rijn Simple
2-Van Rijn Full
3-Wu, Wang & Jia (wil be used with multiple grain size distribution)

Turbulent Prandtl-Schmidt Number (p):
A coefficient influencing the sediment diffusion in flood water.
Influence is inversly linear (Diffusion *= 1/p)

Grain size distribution (not active for now)

  • Estimate the distribution using a Log-normal function and the D50 and D90 values.
  •  Provide a custom distribution trough a list of maps
