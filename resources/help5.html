Erosion options

Splash detachment

Rainfall kinetic energy: the equations describe non-linear relations between rainfall intensity (mm/h) and kinetic energy (J/mm):

  • A negative exponential equation with average global parameters (van Dijk 2002). values are a= 28.3, b =0.52, c=0.042.
  • A USLE type erquation with paraneters as found in the EUROSEM model (Morgan et al., 1998). a = 8.950, b=8.440
  • A decreasing power law (Sanchez, 2012). a = 7.6, b = 0.22, This equation is available in an nintensity based and timebased version.

Splash delivery is the fraction of sediment splashed from dry parts in a cell to ponded areas in a cell. The division of dry and ponded parts is calculated from the surface roughness and water height. For large gridcells use a small value to prevent overestimation of this effect (splash distances are 50 cm at most).

Splash equation in Lisem aggregate stability is the median drop size to halve an aggregate (Lowe test): higher stability means less detachment. In Eurosem Aggregate stability is the opposite: it is the sediment delivery in g/J. Higher values mean more detachment!

Flow detachment

Detachment efficiency based on soil cohesion (in kPa): efficiency is a value from 0 - 1 multiplied with the detachment rate (depends on soil cohesion in kPa). 0 gives no flow detachment, 1 gives max flow detachment. openLLISEM is very sensitive to the efficiency. Generally 1 gives the most detachment  and 2 the lowest detachment.

  1. Y = 1.0/(0.89+0.56*Cohesion)  (original LISEM model, for silty loams)
  2. Y = 0.79*exp(-0.85*Cohesion) (EUROSEM model)
  3. Y = 1.0/(2.0*Cohesion) (Morgan, Morgan and Finney model)

NOTE: eq 1 gives the highest flow detachment and is for fine material eq 2 the lowest, eq 3 in between the two.

NOTE: a negative cohesion value in coh.map or chancoh.map gives zero detachment (useful for concrete channels for instance)

Two phase sediment transport

Sediment is simulated in two layers: suspended and bedload. Transport capacities can be different for each layer, see below. The water depth is divided into a bedload layer depth and a suspended layer depth. Suspended transport is based on the D50 (median) value of the grainsize distribution, bedload on the upper D90 (upper 90% grainsize). Detachment and deposition are reported as the sum of the two.

For the channel and the surface flow (overland flow and flood) the transport cvapacities and turbulence parameters can be selected separately.

Bed load transport capacity equation:
     1-Van Rijn Simplified (2007)
     2-Van Rijn Full (1984)

Suspended load transport capacity:
     0-Govers et al. (1990)
     1-Van Rijn Simplified (2007)
     2-Van Rijn Full (1984)

Turbulent Prandtl-Schmidt Number (p): a coefficient influencing the sediment diffusion in flood water (def.=0.5). Influence is inversly linear (Diffusion *= 1/p).
